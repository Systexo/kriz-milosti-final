Visit Us

What to expect?

Visiting a new church can be a bit overwhelming, we know. However, we hope this visit is no stress, and easy for you and your family to navigate.

Our whole service is centered on the Gospel of Grace, with a particular view of Christ and his work on the cross to bring salvation to sinners and sinners into the Kingdom of God. We will sing the good news, pray the good news, preach the good news, testify to the good news, and partake of the Lord’s supper (a reminder of the good news) each week.

During our singing you’ll hear a mix of old and new hymns, English and Czech songs, all of them rooted in the classical reformed understanding of Christ’s completed work on the cross.

If you have kids, that’s great. Currently our services are in the Corpus family’s house, or in the garden weather permitting. They have a toddler living with them and have all sorts of things to keep little ones engaged. If your kids are a bit older,. that’s great, they are welcome in the service as well, we love to hear kids getting loud and moving around during the service. It’s a reminder that the Lord saves families, and brings them into his family.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some answers to most common questions

What should I wear?

Wear what you want, no judgment. You can wear shorts in the summer, dresses, pants, slacks, jeans, sweats, whatever… really. We won’t judge you, and ask that you don’t judge others for their clothing choices either.

When should I arrive?

10:30 AM. We are located a short five minute walk from Ládví Metro station. There are also bus and tram links available at Ládví. We start at 10:30, and end around 12:00. At the conclusion of the service we invite you to Stay and enjoy the Lord’s supper and then lunch together. Bring something for lunch, but the bread and wine will be provided.

Where do I go?

You can message Vince on WhatsApp to get the exact address.

Is there parking nearby?

Yes, there is street parking in from of the house, and on weekends it is free.

How do I get in?

If the gate is not open, ring the buzzer.
